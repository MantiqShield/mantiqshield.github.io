The idea, the moat

Part 0 — The idea, in full

Everything discussed, in one place

MantiqShield is a locally-run compliance engine that reads a UAE company's transactions, checks every one against a versioned copy of actual UAE tax law, and says — before the transaction happens, not months later at filing time — exactly which article it violates, what it will cost, and how to fix it.

It exists because in June 2023 the UAE ended roughly fifty years of near-zero taxation and layered a 9% federal corporate tax on top of a patchwork of 40-plus free zones, each running its own qualifying-income rules, on top of three separate legal traditions (UAE civil law on the mainland, English common law inside DIFC and ADGM). A company that rents an office in DMCC, banks with a mainland-regulated entity, and sells to a client in Abu Dhabi Global Market is now required to know which of those jurisdictions' rules apply to which transaction — and get it right continuously, not once a year. Almost nobody can hold that matrix in their head, including most of the accountants serving these businesses.

The problem — a threshold trap, invisible until it fires

One AED 200,000 invoice, five years of consequences

The single most dangerous rule in the new regime is the de minimis threshold for Qualifying Free Zone Persons (QFZP). A free-zone entity keeps its 0% tax rate only as long as its "non-qualifying" revenue — broadly, mainland-sourced income — stays under the lower of 5% of total revenue or AED 5,000,000 in a tax period. Cross that line even once, even by accident, and the entity loses QFZP status not just for that period but for the four subsequent tax periods (Cabinet Decision No. 100 of 2023, Art. 4). One routine invoice, evaluated in isolation, looks completely unremarkable. Its cumulative effect against a running balance most finance teams do not track in real time is what actually causes the damage.

Ordinary compliance failures stack on top: AED 10,000 for late corporate-tax registration, AED 500–1,000 per month for late filing, and 14% per annum on unpaid tax. Origin

From a cloud pitch to a sovereign product — and why that's a feature

The idea started an agentic RegTech platform envisioned as a cloud-hosted multi-agent network wired continuously into a company's ERP and bank accounts, marketed on "data gravity" — once you're plugged into a client's live financial systems, switching cost becomes prohibitive.

What actually got built, MantiqShield, kept the technical thesis — ground every decision in a verified legal corpus, never let a language model touch the arithmetic, cite the exact law article behind every verdict — but changed the delivery model entirely. Instead of a cloud service, it is a AIRGAPPED SYSTEM, licensed per entity with signed credit refills instead of a subscription database call. MantiqShield reflects that shift: an on-premise shield, not a cloud watchdog.

This is not a compromise, it's a sequencing decision. Sovereign, fully-offline deployment removes the single objection that kills most Gulf RegTech sales conversations before they start — the data-residency question — instantly and for free. It survives an enterprise security review, because there is nothing on a shared server for a reviewer to interrogate. And because the same connectors (Xero, Zoho, CSV, PINT-AE e-invoicing) still give continuous ingestion running locally, the original "data gravity" switching-cost logic survives — it just accumulates on the client's own machine instead of on ours. The moat travels with the deployment; it just doesn't require us to hold the data to get it.

How it works

A deterministic pipeline, not a chatbot
StageWhat happens
InputInvoice photos, CSV exports, PINT-AE e-invoices, ERP feeds arrive from the connector layer

Deterministic engineEvery AED figure is computed in Python Decimal against a versioned legal corpus — QFZP de minimis, Small Business Relief, VAT thresholds, the penalty schedule
Cited verdictCompliant / flagged / blocked, tax due, and the exact law article the verdict relies on — appended to an immutable, append-only audit trail

Three invariants make this defensible rather than "GPT with a system prompt":

No LLM arithmetic. Every number that appears in a report comes from deterministic code with explicit rounding rules. The language model's only jobs are reading documents (vision extraction) and phrasing mitigation text — it never computes a figure. This directly answers the fear that motivated the whole idea in the first place: a generic model hallucinating one free-zone clause can cost a client millions, so the arithmetic simply never touches a model that could hallucinate it.

Privacy by construction. PII is pseudonymised before any model sees a document, and combined with fully-local deployment, a client's financial data never leaves their own machine. There is no cloud copy to breach and no data-residency question to answer.

No uncited claims. Retrieval runs over a versioned corpus of the actual legal instruments — decrees, cabinet decisions, free-zone rules — with supersession tracking (Cabinet Decision 55 of 2023 superseded by 100 of 2023 is tracked explicitly, not silently overwritten). A verdict without an article citation does not ship.

The live console — real engine output against a 75-transaction seeded demo ledger, not a mockup. Every row is a cited verdict; the right panel drills into any transaction's tax basis and legal reasoning.

The pre-flight intercept — the actual BLOCK verdict for the worked example above, captured live from the running app.

The moat

Three layers, each hard to copy for a different reason
01

Verified legal corpus

Article-level chunks of the actual legal instruments, with effective dates, provenance flags, and supersession chains. Keeping it current is a full-time editorial asset, currently undergoing practitioner sign-off instrument by instrument.

A generic LLM hallucinating one free-zone clause costs a client millions. This engine cannot cite what isn't in the corpus.

02

Sovereign by architecture

Node-locked binary, fully offline, signed credit refills. Client financial data never leaves the client's machine — there is no cloud to trust and no data-residency review to survive.

Cloud-based competitors need UAE data-residency approval just to start a sales conversation. This skips that conversation entirely.

03

Immutable audit trail

Every verdict appends to a tamper-evident local ledger — score, figures, citations, trace. Months of compliance evidence accumulate inside each deployment.

Switching means abandoning your own audit history. Data gravity, without us ever holding the data.
